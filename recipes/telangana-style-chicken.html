  1. 1 kg Chicken (on the bone, skinless, cut into medium pieces)
  2. 2 tbsp Vegetable Oil
  3. 300 gms White Onion (finely chopped)
  4. 2‚Äù Ginger (roughly chopped, for paste)
  5. 8 cloves Garlic (roughly chopped, for paste)
  6. 15 Curry Leaves (divided)
  7. 1 tsp Turmeric Powder
  8. ¬º tsp Kashmiri Chilli Powder (or mild paprika)
  9. 150 mls Water
  10. Juice of half a lemon
  11. Coriander (for garnish)
  12. 120 gms Freshly Grated Coconut (or desiccated coconut)
For the Spice Powder:
  1. 6 Dried Mild Red Chillies (deseeded)
  2. 2 tbsp Coriander Seeds
  3. 1 tbsp Cumin Seeds
  4. 1 tbsp Black Peppercorn
  5. 1 heaped tbsp White Poppy Seeds (Khus Khus)
Step-by-Step Instructions Prepare the Spices and Pastes:
  1. Roast Whole Spices: Heat a dry frying pan on low heat. Add the dried chillies, coriander seeds, cumin seeds, and black peppercorns. Roast for 8-10 minutes, shaking often.
  2. Add Poppy Seeds: Add the poppy seeds and stir well for 2-3 minutes. Take the pan off the heat and cool completely.
  3. Grind to Powder: Grind the cooled roasted spices to a fine powder in a spice grinder and set aside.
  4. Make Coconut Paste: In the same pan, add the grated coconut and dry fry for 10 minutes, stirring well. Cool, then add to a grinder with 50 mls of water and grind to a smooth paste. Set aside.
  5. Make Ginger-Garlic Paste: Grind the roughly chopped ginger and garlic to a smooth paste and set aside.
¬† Cook the Curry:
  1. Saut√© Onions: Heat the oil in a heavy-bottomed, large saucepan. Add the chopped onions and fry on medium heat for about 20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they turn a lovely caramel color.
  2. Add Ginger-Garlic & Curry Leaves: Lower the heat and add the ginger-garlic paste. Fry for 2 minutes and stir well. Add half of the curry leaves along with the coconut paste. Fry on medium heat for 4-5 minutes, adding a couple of tablespoons of water if needed to prevent sticking.
  3. Add Chicken and Spices: Add the cleaned chicken, turmeric powder, paprika/chilli powder, and the blended spice powder. Season with salt to taste. Stir well to seal the chicken for about 4 minutes.
  4. Simmer: Add the water, stirring well to scrape any bits from the bottom of the pan. Turn the heat to low and simmer with the lid on for about 20 minutes, stirring halfway through until the chicken is tender and well-cooked.
  5. Finish and Serve: Stir in the lemon juice, coriander, and the remaining curry leaves. Serve the hot Telangana Chicken Curry with roti or rice.
