Muddha Pappu Recipe

Muddha Pappu, also known as Kandi Pappu, is a traditional and comforting lentil dish from the Telugu cuisine of South India. Made with toor dal (pigeon peas), this simple yet flavorful recipe is a staple in many households. It is often enjoyed with steamed rice and a variety of side dishes, making it a wholesome and satisfying meal.

Ingredients and Instructions:

Ingredients Needed for Muddha Pappu:

  1. 1 cup Toor Dal (Kandi pappu or yellow lentils)
  2. 3 cups Water
  3. Salt to taste
  4. Can add Turmeric powder
  5. 2 tbsp Ghee (clarified butter)
Step-by-Step Instructions

Prepare the Dal:

  1. Wash the toor dal thoroughly multiple times under running water.
  2. Pressure Cook: Place the washed dal in a pressure cooker and add the water. For an optional extra aroma, you can dry roast the dal lightly before washing.
  3. Cook: Close the pressure cooker lid and cook for about 3-5 whistles on medium-high heat, then simmer on low for a few minutes. The goal is to have the dal completely cooked and mashable.
  4. Release Pressure: Allow the pressure to release naturally. Do not open the cooker lid until the pressure has fully subsided.
  5. Mash and Season: Open the cooker and add salt to the cooked dal. Use a ladle or a wooden masher to mash the dal well until it reaches a thick, creamy consistency. If it is too thick, you can add a little hot water to adjust the consistency.
  6. Add Ghee and Serve: Stir in the ghee (clarified butter) and mix well. Serve the hot Mudda Pappu immediately with steamed rice and your favorite mango pickle or a tangy Pulusu stew.
